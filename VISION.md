# 🔮 Aries Vision — Community-Powered AI

> This document describes the long-term vision for Aries. These are goals we're working toward, not features that exist today. We believe in being transparent about what's aspirational vs. what's shipped.

---

## The Idea

Most AI tools are wrappers around corporate APIs. You pay a subscription, your data flows through someone else's servers, and you have zero control over the model. Aries aims to change that equation.

### Compute-for-AI Exchange

The core concept: people contribute idle compute power to a shared network and earn access to AI capabilities in return.

```
Your PC (idle) ──── contributes compute ────▶ Swarm Network
                                                  │
Your PC        ◀──── AI access (tokens) ─────────┘
```

- **High-end GPU** → Contributes to distributed training and inference → earns AI tokens
- **Mid-range CPU** → Handles lighter workloads → earns AI tokens
- **Everyone together** → More compute = better model for all

### Community-Trained Model

Instead of relying on a single corporation's model, Aries envisions a model that improves through community contribution:

- Distributed fine-tuning across swarm nodes
- Continuous improvement as more people join
- No single point of failure
- Community-owned, not corporate-owned

### Why It Matters

| Current Reality | What We're Building Toward |
|---|---|
| Monthly subscriptions | Earn access by contributing compute |
| Corporate-controlled models | Community-trained and community-owned |
| Your data on their servers | 100% local, always |
| Service disappears = you lose access | Decentralized, no single point of failure |

## Current State

The swarm networking infrastructure exists in Aries today (P2P discovery, pairing, task distribution). The distributed training and token economy are still in development. We're being honest about that.

If this vision interests you, [join the conversation](https://github.com/dsfgsdgf33/aries/issues) or contribute to the codebase.
